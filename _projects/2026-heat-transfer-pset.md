Reflection:

I found problem set #4 most valuable/educational. In problem 1 of this pset, I learned how to apply the thermal resistance method to a real life example (silicon PV panel). Specifically, I applied thermal resistances to model different modes of heat transfer (conduction, convection, and radiation) in series and parallel to determine the temperature of various important locations. Many heat transfer applications involve multiple materials and modes of heat transfer, so being able to model these different heat transfers as a thermal resistance network will be useful in analyzing practical heat transfer systems. In problems 2 and 3, I learned how to analyze the temperature along a fin with adiabatic and non-adiabatic tip conditions. I was able to model the fin with a convective tip condition as a fin with an adiabatic tip condition by modifying the length of the fin according to Lc = L + t/2. I also calculated the thermal resistance and effectiveness of the fin. This helped quantify how a fin can improve a structure's ability to transfer heat, which explains why fins are commonly used in real life applications to aid the dissipation of heat. It was also useful to practice modeling a non-adiabatic tip as adiabatic, since real life applications often are not perfectly insulated at the tip.
